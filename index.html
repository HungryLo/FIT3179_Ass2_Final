Economies Around the World

The map above shows GDP per Capita across the world.

We observe that United States and Australia are some of the richest countries in the world.

Africa and South America are some of the poorer regions in the world.

Furthermore, the poorest countries are generally land-locked.

GDP Breakdown

The RICHEST continent in the world is North America, followed by Europe. Over the years, each region's GDP has increased significantly, except for Africa. Some possible reasons include corrupt leaders and civil unrest. The Financial Crisis in 2008 had caused the GDP of several regions to drop sharply.

For all of the countries above, the service industry is the major contributor to its GDP, followed by industrial and agricultural. Agricultural industry tends to contribute less than 3% to a country's GDP, with the exception of China (7%) and India (17%) .

China and US are the two greatest economies in the world. Both countries spend a similar amount (A difference of 5%) for categories such as transportation, general government and and social security.

China spends a lot more on education while US spends a lot more on healthcare. Additionally, US has the highest military budget in the world.

The Effects of GDP

Poorer countries tend to have a shorter life expectancy while richer countries tend to have a longer life expectancy.

However, it is worth nothing that a large difference in GDP does not necessarily translate to a large difference in life expectancy.

In other words, there are other factors other than GDP that contribute to life expectancy.

Some of those factors include gender, diet, genetics, lifestyle, exercise, crime rate and others.
